Sense & Size

A playful journey to build early number sensitivity... Learn more by clicking here

This game helps young children (ages 3-6) develop an early understanding of quantity. Rather than focusing directly on counting, it seeks to build a strong foundation through visual differences such as size, color intensity, and spacing.

Perceptual discrimination—a key early skill in the development of numerical cognition—is the ability to notice and compare differences between objects. Through playful practice, this game helps children strengthen that ability.

The approach is inspired by the Signal Clarity Hypothesis, which suggests that children learn about numbers more effectively when the differences are perceptually clear, rather than relying on abstract symbols. By emphasizing continuous magnitudes (like cumulative area, density, and contrast), the game supports the natural developmental pathway from intuitive quantity perception to more formal numerical understanding.
